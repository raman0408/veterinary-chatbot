Dairy Cattle Health and Management Knowledge Base

1. Mastitis: A common infection of the udder. Symptoms include a swollen, warm udder and clotted or watery milk. Prevent by maintaining strict hygiene during milking.

2. Feeding Milking Cows: An average milking cow requires 20-25 kg of green fodder, 4-5 kg of dry fodder, and 2-3 kg of concentrate mixture daily for optimal milk production.

3. Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD): A highly contagious viral disease. Symptoms include high fever, reduced milk yield, and blisters in the mouth and on the hooves. It is prevented through bi-annual vaccination.

4. Calf Dehorning: To prevent future injuries, calves should be dehorned within the first 15 to 20 days of birth.

5. Water Intake: A healthy, lactating dairy cow needs to drink between 115 to 190 liters of fresh water per day depending on the climate and milk yield.
